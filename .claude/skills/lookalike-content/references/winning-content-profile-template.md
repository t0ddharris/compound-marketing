```markdown

## Based on: [N] posts analysed ([N] top performers, [N] baseline, [N] low performers)

## Data source: [Description of what was uploaded]

[One paragraph summarising what makes this content work. This should be specific enough that someone could read just this paragraph and immediately know what kind of content to create.]

- **Structure type:** [single-point vs. multi-point, list vs. prose]
- **Paragraph style:** [short/long, consistent/varied]

The opening patterns that work:

- [Move 1 with evidence]

---

## 10. The Winning Formula

**One sentence:** The content that works for this audience is [format] about [topics], opening with [hook style], using [emotional register], at [length], with [specificity level].

**Testable checklist:**
- [ ] Topic matches one of the winning clusters?
- [ ] Format matches the primary format?
- [ ] Hook follows the winning hook pattern?
- [ ] Emotional register matches?
- [ ] Length is in the winning range?
- [ ] Specificity level is calibrated correctly?
- [ ] Close follows the winning close pattern?
```

## Rules for Writing the Profile

**Be specific, not generic.** "Posts about implementation challenges" is useful. "Posts about relevant topics" is not.

**Reference the actual data.** Every finding should point to specific posts from the data dump as evidence.

**Distinguish between patterns and coincidences.** A pattern needs at least 3 posts showing the same thing. Two posts is a coincidence — note it but don't build recommendations on it.

**If metrics exist, compare top vs. bottom.** The most useful findings come from what's different between high and low performers, not just what the high performers have in common.

**If no metrics exist, look for recurring patterns.** Without performance data, you're looking for consistency — what the creator does repeatedly, which likely reflects what their audience responds to (or they wouldn't keep doing it).
