## 1. AI Slop Sentence Patterns (Hard Ban)

These are dead giveaways for AI-generated content. Zero tolerance — rewrite every instance.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "X wasn't Y. It was Z." | "The query wasn't slow. It was blocked." |
| "That's not just X. That's Y." | "That's not just detection. That's prevention." |
| "Here's the thing:" | — |
| "Here's why that matters:" | — |
| "Let that sink in." | — |
| "Read that again." | — |
| "Full stop." | — |
| "And that changes everything." | — |

**What to do:** Rewrite in a natural, direct voice. State the point without rhetorical scaffolding.

Pull the specific list from `brain/positioning-and-messaging.md` (the "words we avoid" / messaging guardrails section) and scan for those terms here. These are words that weaken the company's messaging or are too generic for the category. Common categories to watch:

## 3. AI Writing Tics (Hard Ban / Limit)

From `skills/copy-editing/SKILL.md`.

### Rhetorical Negation-Pivot (Limit: 1 per piece)

Pattern: "X isn't Y. It's Z." / "X wasn't Y. It was Z." / "That's not just X. That's Y."

- **One** instance maximum, and only when it's genuinely the strongest way to frame the core argument
- If you find more than one, keep the strongest and rewrite the rest into direct positive statements

### Throat-Clearing Constructions (Hard Ban)

- "Here's the thing:" / "Here's why that matters:" / "Here's where this gets [adjective]:"
- "Let that sink in." / "Read that again." / "Full stop."
- "And that changes everything."

### Repetitive Rhythm Patterns (Hard Ban)

- Multiple short declarative sentences in a row without payoff (e.g., "X is happening. Y is growing. Z is changing.")
- Any rhetorical device used more than once in the same piece — if you used the same construction twice, one of them goes

## 7. AI Opening Phrases (Hard Ban)

From `skills/seo-geo/references/ai-writing-detection.md`.

### Openings
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In today's digital age..."
- "In an era of..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Let's delve into..."
- "Imagine a world where..."

### Transitions
- "That being said..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "At its core..."
- "To put it simply..."
- "In essence..."
- "This begs the question..."

### Conclusions
- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "By [doing X], you can [achieve Y]..."
- "In the final analysis..."
- "All things considered..."
- "At the end of the day..."

### Structural Patterns
- "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..." (listing three examples after "whether")
- "It's not just [X], it's also [Y]..."
- "Think of [X] as [elaborate metaphor]..."
- Starting sentences with "By" followed by a gerund: "By understanding X, you can Y..."

## 10. Em Dash Overuse (Limit: max 3 per blog post)

From `skills/seo-geo/references/ai-writing-detection.md`.

Em dashes (—) are one of the most reliable markers of AI-generated content. AI models overuse them as a shortcut for sentence variety.

**Rule:** Maximum 3 em dashes per blog post. Each one must be a deliberate choice.

| Instead of em dashes | Use |
|---------------------|-----|
| Parenthetical info | Commas or parentheses |
| Introducing explanations | Colons |
| Adding supplementary info | Parentheses |
| Emphasis | Restructure the sentence |

## Quick-Scan Process

During Step 4 (Quality Enforcement), scan in this order:

1. **Ctrl+F for hard bans:** Search for "Here's the thing", "Let that sink in", "Read that again", "Full stop", "changes everything", "wasn't", "isn't"/"It's" pairs, "not just"/"That's" pairs
2. **Ctrl+F for overused words:** delve, leverage, utilize, robust, comprehensive, seamless, innovative, cutting-edge, furthermore, moreover
3. **Ctrl+F for em dashes:** Count "—" occurrences. If >3, replace excess with commas, colons, or parentheses
4. **Read opening paragraph:** Check against AI Opening Phrases list
5. **Read closing paragraph:** Check against AI Conclusion Phrases list
6. **Scan for filler words:** very, really, just, basically, actually, incredibly
7. **Check brand compliance:** Verify the draft uses the company's approved terms and framing from `brain/positioning-and-messaging.md`, not generic category language
